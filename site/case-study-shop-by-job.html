Shop by Job

20% increase in transaction completion rates — a purchase flow designed around how Pro contractors actually think.

Lowe's India

UX Research Product Design Usability Testing

The Challenge


Pro contractors think in terms of jobs, not individual products. The existing Lowe's purchase experience was built around individual item search and add-to-cart — a consumer mental model that didn't match how Pros actually plan and buy materials.

Pros were abandoning mid-flow or working around the system using spreadsheets and manual lists. The challenge was to design a purchase flow that matched their job-based mental model.


Pros think in jobs, not products — the purchase flow had to reflect that.

Research & Discovery

User interviews with Pro contractors Workflow observation sessions Competitive benchmarking Analytics review of drop-off points

I conducted contextual research with Pro customers to understand their purchasing patterns, job planning behavior, and pain points with the current experience.

Key insight: Pros organize purchases by job type (e.g., bathroom remodel, deck build) and need to quickly replicate past orders. The job context — not the product catalog — was the natural starting point.


Contextual interviews revealed that every Pro's buying decision starts with a named job — not a product search.

The Solution

Job-first purchase flow Saved job templates Reorder from past jobs Pro checkout

A job-first purchase flow where contractors select or create a job, get a pre-populated materials list for that job type, adjust quantities, and complete checkout — all without navigating the standard product catalog.

Usability Testing

Three rounds of usability testing with Pro customers.

Each round tested a specific stage of the flow: job-type selection and taxonomy in the first round, materials list review and editing in the second, and cart confirmation and order summary in the third. Design was iterated between rounds based on observed friction and direct participant feedback.

The job-type taxonomy was restructured after round one — the initial categories had been defined around internal product logic, not the language contractors used naturally. The second round tested a revised taxonomy built from terms participants used unprompted. Completion and confidence improved measurably.

The Result


20% increase in transaction completion rates for Pro customers.

Post-launch data showed a measurable reduction in cart abandonment among Pro users and positive qualitative feedback around the job-based flow feeling "natural" and "how I actually work." The feature became a foundation for subsequent Pro-specific enhancements on the platform.
